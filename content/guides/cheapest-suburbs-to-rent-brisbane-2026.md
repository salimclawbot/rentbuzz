## Brisbane Rental Market Overview 2026

Brisbane's rental market in 2026 is shaped by several colliding forces: continued interstate migration from New South Wales and Victoria, a construction sector constrained by labour shortages, early-stage Olympics infrastructure works, and interest rate settings that have kept many would-be buyers renting for longer.

According to CoreLogic's March 2026 Hedonic Home Value Index, Brisbane's median weekly rent for all dwellings sits at approximately $580/week, up from around $530/week at the same point in 2025. That represents roughly a 9.4% year-on-year increase, slower than the double-digit growth seen in 2023 and 2024 but still well above the long-run average.

### Key market statistics

| Metric | March 2026 | March 2025 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Median weekly rent (all dwellings) | $580 | $530 | +9.4% |
| Median weekly rent (houses) | $620 | $565 | +9.7% |
| Median weekly rent (units) | $500 | $460 | +8.7% |
| Vacancy rate (Greater Brisbane) | 1.3% | 1.1% | +0.2pp |
| Total rental listings | ~12,400 | ~10,800 | +14.8% |

The vacancy rate has inched up from the crisis-level lows of 2023-2024 when it dipped below 0.8% across large parts of the city. At 1.3%, it remains a landlord's market, but renters have slightly more breathing room than they did eighteen months ago.

For a broader look at how Brisbane compares to other capitals, see our guide on the [cheapest suburbs to rent in Sydney in 2026](/guides/cheapest-suburbs-to-rent-sydney-2026).

### Where rents are still affordable

Despite the headline numbers, significant price variation exists across Greater Brisbane. While inner-ring suburbs like New Farm, Paddington, and Bulimba command median rents of $700-$900/week for houses, outer suburbs in the Logan, Moreton Bay, and Ipswich corridors still offer two-bedroom units in the $350-$420/week range.

The key for budget-conscious renters is knowing exactly where to look and being prepared to move quickly. If you need help with the application process, our guide on [how to improve your rental application](/guides/how-to-improve-your-rental-application) covers the essentials.

## How We Ranked the Cheapest Suburbs

Our rankings draw on data from Domain.com.au, SQM Research, and the Residential Tenancies Authority (RTA) Queensland bond lodgement records for the twelve months to March 2026. We filtered for suburbs with at least 30 active rental listings over the period to ensure statistical reliability.

Each suburb was assessed across four dimensions:

1. **Median weekly rent** -- the primary ranking factor, covering both two-bedroom units and three-bedroom houses
2. **Transport access** -- proximity to train stations, busway stops, or high-frequency bus routes, scored 1-5
3. **Livability** -- access to shops, schools, parks, healthcare facilities, and employment nodes, scored 1-5
4. **Rent trend** -- whether rents are stable, rising slowly, or surging, which affects future affordability

## Cheapest Suburbs on Brisbane's Southside

Brisbane's southside and the adjacent Logan City Council area contain the highest concentration of affordable rentals in the metro area. These suburbs benefit from train line access, established shopping centres, and relatively short commute times to the CBD.

### Woodridge

Woodridge consistently ranks as one of Brisbane's most affordable rental suburbs. Located in the City of Logan, roughly 25 km south of the CBD, it sits on the Beenleigh train line with direct services to South Brisbane, South Bank, and Central stations.

| Property type | Median weekly rent | YoY change |
|---|---|---|
| 2-bedroom unit | $380 | +5.6% |
| 3-bedroom house | $460 | +7.0% |

- **Transport access:** 4/5 -- Woodridge train station on the Beenleigh line; high-frequency bus routes along Kingston Road
- **Livability:** 3/5 -- Logan Central shopping precinct nearby; several primary schools; Chatswood Hills Reserve for green space
- **Rent trend:** Moderate growth; still among the cheapest in Greater Brisbane

Woodridge's affordability is partly driven by its reputation, which has improved markedly over the past decade thanks to community investment and improved infrastructure. For renters focused purely on value, it remains hard to beat.

### Inala

Inala is located 16 km southwest of the CBD in the Brisbane City Council area. It is one of the few suburbs within Brisbane proper -- not an adjacent LGA -- where rents remain genuinely affordable.

| Property type | Median weekly rent | YoY change |

| 3-bedroom house | $470 | +8.0% |

- **Transport access:** 3/5 -- no train station, but well-served by bus routes to Indooroopilly and the CBD via the Ipswich Motorway corridor
- **Livability:** 3/5 -- Inala Civic Centre; Inala Plaza shopping centre; strong multicultural food scene; multiple schools
- **Rent trend:** Rising faster than Woodridge due to spillover demand from Richlands and Forest Lake

Inala's proximity to the Centenary Motorway and Ipswich Motorway makes it attractive for renters who drive to western or southern employment hubs.

## Inner-City Units vs Outer-Suburb Houses: A Cost Comparison

One of the biggest decisions for Brisbane renters in 2026 is whether to prioritise location or space. Here is how the numbers stack up.

### The inner-city unit option

Suburbs within 10 km of the CBD -- think Woolloongabba, Kangaroo Point, Fortitude Valley, Spring Hill, and South Brisbane -- offer excellent walkability, nightlife, and employment access. However, rents are steep.

| Suburb | 1-bed unit | 2-bed unit |
|---|---|---|
| Fortitude Valley | $430/wk | $560/wk |
| Woolloongabba | $440/wk | $580/wk |
| South Brisbane | $460/wk | $600/wk |
| Spring Hill | $400/wk | $530/wk |
| Kangaroo Point | $450/wk | $590/wk |

A one-bedroom unit in Spring Hill or Fortitude Valley is the cheapest way to live near the CBD, but you sacrifice space and may deal with noise and older building stock.

### The outer-suburb house option

For the same $460-$500/week that a two-bedroom inner-city unit costs, renters can secure a three-bedroom house with a yard in suburbs like Woodridge, Goodna, or Caboolture.

| Suburb | 3-bed house | Distance to CBD | Train time to CBD |
|---|---|---|---|
| Woodridge | $460/wk | 25 km | ~40 min |
| Goodna | $450/wk | 25 km | ~38 min |
| Caboolture | $465/wk | 50 km | ~65 min |
| Ipswich CBD | $440/wk | 40 km | ~50 min |
| Beenleigh | $475/wk | 35 km | ~50 min |

### The hidden costs of outer suburbs

Cheaper rent does not always mean lower total living costs. Outer-suburb renters should factor in:

- **Transport costs:** A second car may be necessary. Fuel, registration, insurance, and maintenance can add $150-$250/week per vehicle.
- **Commute time:** An hour-plus commute each way has a real cost in time and wellbeing.
- **Tolls:** The Gateway and Logan Motorways charge tolls that add up quickly for daily commuters. A round trip from Beenleigh to the CBD via toll roads can cost $10-$15/day.

## The 2032 Olympics Effect on Brisbane Rents

The 2032 Brisbane Olympic and Paralympic Games cast a long shadow over the city's property and rental markets. While the Games are still six years away, their impact is already being felt.

### Infrastructure projects driving change

Several major infrastructure projects linked to the Olympics are reshaping Brisbane's transport and housing landscape:

- **Cross River Rail** -- completed in 2025, this underground rail line has improved connectivity for southside and inner-city suburbs, but has also pushed up rents in newly accessible areas like Woolloongabba and Boggo Road.
- **Brisbane Metro** -- the rubber-tyred metro system running along dedicated busway corridors has improved service frequency on the Southeast and Northern Busways.
- **Gabba redevelopment** -- the planned rebuild of the Gabba stadium (subject to ongoing review) has created uncertainty in Woolloongabba, with some landlords holding properties off the rental market pending resumption decisions.
- **Brisbane Arena** -- the planned entertainment arena at Roma Street is driving interest in inner-north suburbs like Spring Hill and Herston.
- **Northern corridor upgrades** -- road and rail improvements between Brisbane and the Sunshine Coast are benefiting suburbs like Caboolture and Morayfield.

### Which suburbs are being affected?

Suburbs closest to Olympic venues and infrastructure projects have seen the sharpest rent increases:

| Suburb | Olympic link | Rent impact |
|---|---|---|
| Woolloongabba | Gabba stadium, Cross River Rail station | +12-15% YoY |
| Albion | Proposed aquatics centre precinct | +10-13% YoY |
| Herston | Close to RNA Showgrounds venue precinct | +9-11% YoY |
| Chandler | Sleeman Sports Complex upgrades | +8-10% YoY |
| Redland Bay | Whitewater centre proposals | +6-8% YoY |

Renters looking for affordability should generally look away from these Olympic-impacted zones. The cheapest suburbs profiled in this guide -- Woodridge, Inala, Caboolture, Goodna, Ipswich -- are largely insulated from direct Olympic effects, though they benefit indirectly from improved regional transport.

## Tips for Finding Cheap Rentals in Brisbane

Securing an affordable rental in Brisbane's competitive 2026 market requires strategy. Here are practical tips based on current market conditions.

### Search beyond the obvious platforms

While Domain.com.au and realestate.com.au list the majority of Brisbane rentals, do not overlook:

- **Facebook Marketplace and local community groups** -- private landlords sometimes list here first, often at lower rents to avoid agent fees
- **Flatmates.com.au** -- shared housing can dramatically reduce per-person costs
- **Real estate agency websites directly** -- some smaller agencies list exclusively on their own sites before syndicating to portals
- **University housing boards** -- QUT, UQ, and Griffith all maintain housing databases

### Timing matters

Brisbane's rental market has seasonal patterns. Demand peaks from January to March (start of the university and work year) and again in June-July (mid-year intake). The quietest -- and cheapest -- period to search is typically September to November, when you may find landlords willing to negotiate on price.

### Negotiate on lease length

In a market with rising vacancy rates (even if they are rising from very low levels), landlords may offer a lower weekly rent in exchange for a longer lease commitment. A 12- or 18-month lease signed in a quieter period can lock in a lower rate.

In Brisbane's market, properties in the $350-$450/week range attract dozens of applications. Having a complete application ready -- including 100 points of ID, rental references, payslips, and a cover letter -- makes a significant difference. ### Consider emerging suburbs

Suburbs that are not yet well-known but are benefiting from new infrastructure often offer a window of affordability before prices catch up. In 2026, watch:

- **Burpengary East** -- new housing estates north of Caboolture with three-bedroom houses around $470/week

- **Yarrabilba** -- south of Logan, a large master-planned development with houses from $480/week

Queensland's rental laws were updated with reforms that took effect in 2024, including limits on rent increases to once per twelve months and requirements for landlords to provide reasons for ending tenancies. The Residential Tenancies Authority (RTA) Queensland website at rta.qld.gov.au provides up-to-date information on tenant rights.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the cheapest suburb to rent in Brisbane in 2026?

Woodridge in Logan City is one of Brisbane's cheapest suburbs for renters in 2026, with median weekly rents for two-bedroom units around $380. Other affordable options include Inala ($390/week for a two-bedroom unit), Kingston ($385/week), and Goodna ($370/week). In the Ipswich corridor, the Ipswich CBD area offers two-bedroom units from $365/week.

### Can you rent a two-bedroom unit in Brisbane for under $400 per week?

Yes, several suburbs in Brisbane's south, north, and outer corridors still have two-bedroom units under $400 per week in 2026. Woodridge ($380), Kingston ($385), Inala ($390), Caboolture ($380), Deception Bay ($390), Goodna ($370), Riverview ($375), and the Ipswich CBD ($365) all fall within this range. However, availability at these price points is limited and properties receive high volumes of applications.

### How does Brisbane rent compare to Sydney and Melbourne in 2026?

Brisbane median rents remain significantly lower than Sydney and somewhat lower than Melbourne for comparable properties. A two-bedroom unit that costs $380/week in Woodridge would cost $550+ in equivalent Sydney suburbs like Campbelltown or $470+ in Melbourne suburbs like Dandenong. However, Brisbane rents rose sharply from 2022 to 2025 due to interstate migration and the upcoming 2032 Olympics infrastructure push, narrowing the gap considerably. For Sydney-specific data, see our guide on the [cheapest suburbs to rent in Sydney in 2026](/guides/cheapest-suburbs-to-rent-sydney-2026).

### Will the 2032 Olympics affect Brisbane rental prices?

Olympic infrastructure spending is driving development in areas like Woolloongabba, Albion, and the northern corridor. While this will improve transport and amenities long-term, construction activity has tightened supply in some areas, pushing rents up near Olympic precincts. Suburbs closest to venues have seen 10-15% annual rent growth, compared to 5-8% in outer suburbs. The cheapest suburbs profiled in this guide are largely insulated from direct Olympic impacts.

### Is it better to rent a unit in the city or a house in the outer suburbs?

This depends on your priorities. A two-bedroom inner-city unit at $560/week offers walkability, nightlife, and a short commute but limited space. A three-bedroom outer-suburb house at $460/week provides more space and a yard but requires a longer commute and often a car. When factoring in transport costs, the total cost difference can narrow significantly. Renters who work from home or have flexible arrangements often get the best value from outer-suburb houses.

### What suburbs should renters avoid in Brisbane in 2026?

Rather than avoiding specific suburbs, renters should be cautious about properties in known flood zones (parts of Goodna, Rocklea, and Graceville), areas with active construction disruption near Olympic sites, and suburbs with very low vacancy rates where competition drives rental bidding. Always inspect a property in person, check flood maps, and research the suburb's crime statistics through the Queensland Police Service online portal before applying.
