# Average Rent Perth by Suburb 2026: Complete Price Guide

**By Maya Chen** | Last updated April 2026

![Average rent Perth by suburb 2026 — aerial view of the Perth CBD skyline and Swan River](/images/articles/average-rent-perth-2026-hero.jpg)

**Perth's rental market in 2026 remains one of the tightest in Australia, driven by the sustained mining boom, interstate migration, and critically low vacancy rates hovering near 0.7%. The metro-wide median weekly rent for a three-bedroom house sits at approximately $580, while two-bedroom units average around $480 per week — representing increases of 25-30% since 2022.**

Perth has undergone a dramatic rental transformation over the past four years. What was once considered one of Australia's most affordable capital city markets now rivals Melbourne on median rents, with certain coastal and inner-city suburbs commanding prices that would have seemed implausible just a few years ago. For renters navigating this competitive landscape, understanding how prices vary from suburb to suburb is essential for finding the right home at the right price.

This guide breaks down average rents across every major Perth region — from the CBD and inner suburbs to the northern corridor, southern suburbs, coastal areas, and the eastern hills — giving you the data you need to make informed decisions about where to live.

## Perth Rental Market Overview 2026

Perth's rental market in early 2026 is defined by three converging forces: the ongoing resources boom in Western Australia's north, a sustained wave of interstate and overseas migration, and historically low levels of new rental supply entering the market.

According to REIWA data, the Perth metropolitan vacancy rate sat at just 0.7% in the March 2026 quarter — well below the 2.5-3% range considered balanced. This means renters face intense competition at open inspections, often submitting applications against dozens of other prospective tenants.

### Metro-Wide Median Rents (March 2026 Quarter)

| Property Type | Median Weekly Rent | Annual Change |
|---|---|---|
| 3-Bedroom House | $580 | +6.4% |
| 4-Bedroom House | $650 | +5.7% |
| 2-Bedroom Unit | $480 | +7.9% |
| 1-Bedroom Unit | $390 | +8.3% |
| Room (Shared House) | $220–$280 | +10–12% |

Rooms in shared houses have seen some of the sharpest increases in proportional terms, reflecting the squeeze on younger renters and FIFO workers seeking affordable accommodation close to transport links.

The rental price growth has been broad-based across Perth, but some regions have outpaced others. Inner-city and coastal suburbs have seen the strongest absolute dollar increases, while outer suburbs — though still more affordable — have experienced higher percentage growth as renters are pushed further from the city centre.

## Perth CBD and Inner Suburbs

Perth's CBD and the ring of inner suburbs within five to eight kilometres of the city centre remain the most expensive rental zone. These areas attract professionals, students attending UWA and Curtin University, and workers who value walkability and proximity to employment hubs.

### Perth CBD

The CBD itself is dominated by apartment living. One-bedroom apartments in Perth's central towers average $420 per week, while two-bedroom units sit at $550. Purpose-built build-to-rent developments completed in 2024 and 2025 have added some stock, but occupancy rates remain above 95%.

### Northbridge and Highgate

Adjacent to the CBD, Northbridge and Highgate offer a mix of older character apartments and newer developments. Northbridge's proximity to restaurants, bars, and cultural venues keeps demand high. Two-bedroom units average $510 per week, while one-bedroom apartments start from $380.

### Subiaco

Subiaco has long been one of Perth's most desirable inner-western suburbs. A three-bedroom house in Subiaco commands a median rent of $720 per week, making it one of the priciest suburbs in the metro area. Two-bedroom units average $530.

### East Perth and Rivervale

East Perth offers a more accessible entry point to inner-city living, with two-bedroom apartments averaging $490 per week. Rivervale, just across the causeway, provides slightly more affordable options at $460 for a two-bedroom unit and $540 for a three-bedroom house.

## Northern Suburbs

Perth's northern suburbs stretch from the inner ring at Stirling and Scarborough out to the rapidly growing corridor around Joondalup and beyond. This region offers a wide range of rental price points and has seen substantial population growth over the past decade.

### Stirling and Scarborough

Stirling is a mid-range option at around $560 for a three-bedroom house. Scarborough, which has undergone a significant beachside redevelopment, commands higher prices — $620 for houses and $500 for two-bedroom units — reflecting its lifestyle appeal and proximity to the coast.

### Joondalup

Joondalup functions as a secondary CBD for Perth's northern corridor, with its own train station, Lakeside Joondalup shopping centre, and the Joondalup Health Campus. Three-bedroom houses average $540 per week, while two-bedroom units sit at $430. The suburb attracts families and professionals who work locally or commute to the CBD via the Joondalup line.

### Wanneroo and Clarkson

Further north, Wanneroo and Clarkson offer more affordable options. Three-bedroom houses in Wanneroo average $500, while Clarkson sits at $480. These suburbs have benefited from the extension of the Mitchell Freeway and improved public transport links, making them viable commuting options.

## Southern Corridor

Perth's southern suburbs extend from Victoria Park and Cannington through to Rockingham and Mandurah at the metropolitan fringe. This corridor has historically offered some of Perth's most affordable rentals, though prices have risen sharply as demand has pushed south.

### Victoria Park and Cannington

Victoria Park, just across the Swan River from the CBD, has gentrified significantly and now commands three-bedroom house rents of $590. Cannington, home to Westfield Carousel and well-connected by train and bus, sits at $510 for a three-bedroom house.

### Armadale and Gosnells

Armadale and Gosnells remain among Perth's most affordable suburbs. Three-bedroom houses in Armadale average $440 per week, while Gosnells sits at $450. These suburbs offer genuine value for families willing to accept longer commute times — around 45-55 minutes to the CBD by train.

### Rockingham

Rockingham, located about 40 kilometres south of the CBD, has evolved from a holiday town into a significant suburban centre. Three-bedroom houses average $470 per week. The suburb benefits from its coastal location, the Rockingham train station on the Mandurah line, and proximity to the Kwinana industrial strip, which provides local employment.

### Mandurah

At the southern extremity of the Perth metropolitan rail network, Mandurah offers some of the lowest rents in the broader Perth region. Three-bedroom houses average $430 per week, though quality stock is limited and vacancy rates are even tighter than the metro average at 0.5%.

Perth's coastline is one of its greatest drawcards, and coastal suburbs command a significant premium. From Cottesloe in the west to Fremantle in the south-west, these suburbs attract renters willing to pay more for beach proximity and lifestyle.

### Fremantle

Fremantle blends heritage charm, port-side character, and a lively cultural scene. Three-bedroom houses average $640 per week, while two-bedroom units sit at $500. Fremantle's rental market is particularly tight, with a vacancy rate of just 0.4%, driven by limited new supply in the heritage-protected town centre and strong demand from students and professionals.

### City Beach and Floreat

### Hillarys and Sorrento

Further north along the coast, Hillarys and Sorrento provide a slightly more accessible coastal lifestyle. Three-bedroom houses average $580 in Hillarys and $570 in Sorrento, with two-bedroom units around $440-$460.

## Eastern Hills and Foothills

Perth's eastern hills and foothills — the Darling Range suburbs — offer a distinct alternative to the flat coastal and suburban plains. These suburbs provide larger blocks, bushland settings, and cooler summer temperatures, though at the cost of longer commute times and limited public transport.

### Midland

Midland, at the gateway to the hills, is one of Perth's most affordable suburbs. Three-bedroom houses average $450 per week. The suburb has undergone a revitalisation effort centred around the Midland Gate shopping centre and the historic workshops precinct, but rental prices remain well below the metro median.

### Kalamunda and Mundaring

Higher in the hills, Kalamunda and Mundaring attract renters seeking a semi-rural lifestyle within commuting distance of the city. Three-bedroom houses in Kalamunda average $530, while Mundaring sits at $490. Both suburbs suffer from limited public transport connectivity, making car ownership essential.

### Ellenbrook

Technically in the north-eastern corridor rather than the hills, Ellenbrook is a master-planned community that has grown rapidly over the past two decades. Three-bedroom houses average $490 per week. The opening of the Ellenbrook rail extension in late 2024 has significantly improved the suburb's appeal, reducing commute times to the CBD.

## Mining Boom and the Perth Rental Squeeze

Understanding Perth's rental market in 2026 requires understanding the mining boom that has reshaped Western Australia's economy and demographics over the past several years.

### Population Growth and Labour Demand

Western Australia recorded the strongest population growth of any Australian state in 2025, at 2.4% year-on-year, according to Australian Bureau of Statistics data. Much of this growth has been driven by interstate migration from the eastern states and skilled migration from overseas, both fuelled by the resources sector's insatiable demand for workers.

Iron ore, lithium, and critical minerals operations in the Pilbara and Goldfields regions require vast workforces, many of whom are based in Perth on fly-in, fly-out (FIFO) rosters. These workers need housing in Perth during their off-weeks, adding to rental demand without a corresponding increase in permanent population.

### FIFO Housing Demand

FIFO workers have a particular impact on certain suburbs. Areas near Perth Airport — including High Wycombe, Redcliffe, and Belmont — see elevated demand from workers who value proximity to the terminal. Similarly, suburbs along major freeway corridors that provide fast airport access, such as Joondalup (via the Mitchell Freeway) and Rockingham (via the Kwinana Freeway), are popular with FIFO households.

The FIFO effect also inflates the shared housing market. Many single FIFO workers prefer to rent a room rather than maintain a full property, given they may only be in Perth for two weeks out of every four. This has pushed room rents up by 10-12% year-on-year in the suburbs most popular with mining workers.

### Vacancy Rate Crisis

Perth's vacancy rate has remained below 1% for more than three years running, according to SQM Research data. This is far below the 2.5-3% considered a balanced market and even tighter than Sydney's rate of around 1.3%.

The consequences for renters are significant. Open inspections routinely attract 30-50 groups. Properties are often leased within days of listing. Landlords can afford to be highly selective, and rental bidding — while technically discouraged by real estate industry bodies — occurs informally in many cases.

New housing supply has not kept pace with demand. Building approvals in WA, while higher than the post-2020 lows, remain constrained by construction workforce shortages, elevated material costs, and planning approval delays, particularly in infill areas.

### Impact on Rental Affordability

The rental squeeze has pushed Perth's affordability ratios toward concerning levels. According to the Government of Western Australia's Department of Communities, the proportion of WA households experiencing rental stress — defined as spending more than 30% of gross income on rent — has risen from 28% in 2022 to an estimated 35% in early 2026.

Lower-income households, including those in essential services like healthcare, education, and hospitality, have been disproportionately affected. The state government's response has included increased social housing investment and reforms to the Residential Tenancies Act, but these measures take time to filter through to the market.

## Tips for Renting in Perth's Tight Market

Given the intensity of competition in Perth's rental market, preparation and strategy are essential. Here are practical steps that can help you secure a property.

### Prepare Your Application in Advance

Have a complete rental application ready before you begin inspecting properties. This should include proof of income (at least three recent payslips or a letter from your employer), a rental ledger or reference from your current property manager, 100 points of identification, and a completed application form. Many Perth agencies use online platforms like 2Apply or Ignite, so familiarise yourself with these systems.

### Be Flexible on Move-In Dates

Properties that need tenants quickly are often slightly less competitive. If you can be flexible on your move-in date — ideally ready to move within one to two weeks — you gain an advantage over applicants who need a longer lead time.

### Consider Slightly Less Popular Suburbs

The data tables above show significant price variation across Perth's suburbs. A willingness to look at suburbs one or two stations further along the train line, or five to ten minutes further from the beach, can meaningfully reduce both rents and competition. Suburbs like Clarkson, Ellenbrook, and Thornlie offer good amenity at prices well below their more popular neighbours.

### Present Well at Inspections

First impressions matter. Arrive on time, dress neatly, and be courteous to the property manager. In a market where multiple qualified applicants compete for the same property, personal presentation can be a tiebreaker.

For a comprehensive strategy on standing out in a competitive rental market, our guide on [how to win a rental application in Australia](/guides/how-to-win-rental-application-australia-2026) covers everything from documentation to follow-up techniques.

### Explore Room Rentals and Share Housing

If you are priced out of renting a full property, the shared housing market offers a viable alternative. Perth has an active share house scene, particularly in suburbs like Northbridge, Fremantle, Subiaco, and the university precincts. Rooms typically range from $180 to $290 per week including bills, making them significantly cheaper than studio apartments or one-bedroom units.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the average rent in Perth in 2026?

The median weekly rent for a three-bedroom house in Perth is approximately $580 per week in early 2026. Two-bedroom units average around $480 per week. Perth rents have risen significantly since 2022 due to the mining boom and population growth, with increases of 25-30% over the four-year period. Rents vary substantially by suburb, from around $430 per week in the most affordable outer suburbs to over $800 in premium coastal locations like Cottesloe.

### Is Perth cheaper to rent than Sydney or Melbourne?

Perth median rents are lower than Sydney but have reached parity with — and in some cases exceeded — Melbourne in many areas. A three-bedroom house in Perth rents for a median of $580 per week compared to $700 in Sydney and $560 in Melbourne. However, Perth's vacancy rate remains extremely tight at around 0.7%, making competition fierce despite relatively lower headline prices. The practical experience of finding a rental in Perth can be more challenging than in cities with higher headline rents but more available stock.

### What is the cheapest suburb to rent in Perth?

Suburbs like Armadale, Gosnells, Midland, Mandurah, and Rockingham offer some of Perth's lowest median rents, with three-bedroom houses from $430-$470 per week. These suburbs are in Perth's outer southern and eastern corridors. Mandurah offers the lowest absolute rents at $430 per week for a three-bedroom house, though the 55-minute train commute to the CBD is a trade-off. Midland offers better value when commute time is factored in, at $450 per week and just 35 minutes from the city.

### How has the mining boom affected Perth rents?

The ongoing mining boom has driven strong population growth and worker demand, pushing Perth rents up 25-30% since 2022. FIFO worker housing demand particularly affects suburbs near airports and industrial corridors, including High Wycombe, Redcliffe, and suburbs along major freeway routes. Vacancy rates remain at historic lows of 0.7% across the metro area. The mining sector's demand for labour has also attracted interstate and overseas migrants, further increasing pressure on a housing supply that has not kept pace with population growth.

### Where should I rent in Perth if I am a FIFO worker?

FIFO workers commonly choose suburbs near Perth Airport or with fast freeway access. High Wycombe, Redcliffe, and Belmont are popular due to their proximity to the airport terminal. If you are on a roster that has you home for extended blocks, suburbs further out like Joondalup, Rockingham, or Ellenbrook may offer better lifestyle value. Many single FIFO workers opt for room rentals in shared houses, which range from $200-$280 per week depending on location.

### Are Perth rents expected to keep rising?

Most market analysts expect Perth rents to continue growing through 2026 and into 2027, though at a slower pace than the sharp increases seen in 2023-2024. CoreLogic forecasts annual rental growth of 4-6% for Perth in 2026, down from the 8-10% increases recorded in 2024. The key factors to watch are new housing supply pipelines, migration trends, and commodity price movements that affect mining sector employment.
